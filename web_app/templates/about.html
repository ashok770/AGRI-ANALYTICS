Project Methodology & Goal

This application provides an accurate and transparent forecast of crop yields to help farmers and agribusinesses make better resource decisions.

The Problem: The "Black Box" Gap

Traditional machine learning models predict outcomes but fail to explain **why** they reached that conclusion. In agriculture, a farmer needs to know if low yield is due to fertilizer or low rainfall to take corrective action.

Our goal was to solve this **Explainability Gap** by building an analytical application that delivers both a prediction and its root cause analysis.

The Solution: An Explainable ML Pipeline

1. Data Sources

The model was trained on a large historical dataset combining global records for: **Annual Rainfall, Average Temperature, Pesticides Application,** Crop Type, and Location (Area).

2. Core Model

We used the **Random Forest Regressor** model. This is an ensemble method highly effective at capturing complex, non-linear relationships between weather/soil factors and crop yield.

3. Performance

The model achieved an **RÂ² Score of ~0.9875**, meaning it explains over 98% of the variability in crop yield. This demonstrates high predictive accuracy.

Key Analytical Insight (The 'WHY')

Our **Feature Importance** analysis revealed the top global factors driving yield prediction:

  • **#1:** Crop Type (e.g., Potatoes)
  • **#2:** Pesticide Tonnes Applied
  • **#3:** Average Temperature

By highlighting these factors, the app turns a simple prediction into **actionable advice** for resource allocation.
